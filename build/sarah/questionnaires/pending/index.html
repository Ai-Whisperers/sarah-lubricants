When you sent me the catalog, you said the Distribuidor Mayorista column is your cost. But your factory photos from June 16 (the "precio de costo" Gs numbers you sent to your mom) suggest you're getting closer to the Distribuidor column — about 20-25% lower than Mayorista.

Examples I can match: - Bitchie 20ml: Mayorista = Gs 24,400 / Distribuidor = Gs 19,520 / Your factory cost = Gs 19,700 → Distribuidor - Wet Anal 75ml: Mayorista = Gs 22,060 / Distribuidor = Gs 17,648 / Your factory cost = Gs 22,800 → mixed - LUBE Premium 130ml: Mayorista = Gs 22,800 / Distribuidor = Gs 18,240 / Your factory cost = Gs 22,800 → Mayorista - Sexy Pill 4-cap: Mayorista = Gs 34,288 / Distribuidor = Gs 27,432 / Your factory cost = Gs 34,300 → Mayorista

So it looks like some SKUs at Distribuidor, some at Mayorista. Could be: - Negotiation at the factory - Different categories have different margins - Currency fluctuation (4:1 isn't exact) - You got a one-time trip deal

The question: which column of the price list is your real cost? Distribuidor or Distribuidor Mayorista? Or is it a mix per SKU?

Why it matters: The pricing model I'm building uses Mayorista as the baseline. If you're really at Distribuidor for most SKUs, your landed cost drops by ~Gs 5–8K per unit and the 150% margin target becomes viable. If you're really at Mayorista, we need to keep the 30u/30u/30u launch plan and accept that margin has to come down.

You went to the Sexitive factory in Argentina on June 16 and brought back a first batch of products as personal luggage. We want to confirm a few things and surface some risks.

F1. What's in the suitcase right now?

  • ¿Cuántos SKUs distintos trajiste en el viaje?
  • ¿Cuántas unidades aproximadas de cada uno?
  • ¿Es la primera tanda o ya habías traído antes?
  • ¿Te quedó mercadería allá que vas a recoger en otro viaje, o esto es todo?

Why it matters: We need to know your current inventory to plan the listing + marketing. If you have 30+ of the launch trio, you can list immediately. If you have 5 of one and 50 of another, the launch SKU list needs to reflect reality.

F2. How did you declare it at customs?

  • ¿Cuándo pasaste por aduana en el aeropuerto, ¿cómo declaraste la mercadería?
  • ¿Como efectos personales (sin valor comercial) o como importación?
  • ¿Te retuvieron algo o pasó sin revisión?

Why it matters: Each country allows one personal-effects declaration per year without duties. If you declared this as personal, you can do it again next year. If customs opens your suitcase and sees commercial quantities, they could seize the goods + fine you. This affects future trips.

F3. Is this the import model going forward, or just the first one?

  • ¿Vas a seguir trayendo mercadería vos en viajes, o este es solo el primer lote y de ahora en más vas a usar freight forwarder / courier?
  • Si seguís trayendo vos, ¿cada cuánto podés viajar? (1 vez/mes, 1/trimestre, etc.)
  • ¿Cuál es el límite de cantidad por viaje que podés meter sin que sea problemático?

Why it matters: If you're going to keep using the "modelo viajero" approach, we don't need to budget for DINAVISA + despachante in the launch costs. If the next batch is via freight, we do. This single answer re-shapes the entire financial model.

You asked your mom on June 16: "¿Para qué la factura? ¿Y sirve que esté a tu nombre?"

F6. Customs / DINAVISA risk on the in-hand inventory

This is the most important question in this round.

  • ¿La mercadería que tenés ahora pasó por aduana PY sin problema?
  • ¿La podés vender legalmente sin DINAVISA NSO, o técnicamente estás en zona gris?
