## Why this works

### Enki Store advantages (already in place)
- ✅ **RUC 4978694-6** linked to existing IG and operations
- ✅ **20K Instagram followers** (audience asset — see risk #1)
- ✅ **Bio already says "ENVÍOS a todo el país"** = e-com operation exists
- ✅ **DINAPI registration** claimed by Sarah (need verification, see Q6)
- ✅ **Brand recognition** in PY market (Enki is a known IG name)
- ✅ **Cross-sell** into existing Enki customer base

### Kinky sub-brand advantages
- ✅ **Intimate/intimate-wellness positioning** without redoing Enki's broader identity
- ✅ **Instagram filter / hashtag** can route traffic between Enki and Kinky
- ✅ **Discrete product filter** on the e-com site
- ✅ **Sex-positive** naming without the ad-filter triggers of "Kinky Store" (Instagram/Meta keyword filter blocks "kinky" in some contexts)

### What we avoid
- ❌ "Kinky Store" ad-policy risk (Meta blocks ads with "kinky" in copy)
- ❌ Brand re-launch cost (no need to acquire new DINAPI, new domain, new IG)
- ❌ Customer confusion (existing Enki audience doesn't need to relearn)
- ❌ Domain cost (Enki-related domains partly available: `enkystore.com` AVAILABLE per June 22 WHOIS)

## Brand architecture options considered

| Option | Pros | Cons | Decision |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| **A) Enki + Kinky sección** | Single brand, single IG, sub-section. No new DINAPI. Lower cost. | Kinky is just a filter, not own identity. | ✅ CHOSEN |
| B) Enki (parent) + Kinky (sister brand with own IG) | Independent brand build. Kinky can have sex-positive tone. | 2x DINAPI cost. 2x domain cost. Split audience. | ❌ Rejected for v1 |
| C) Kinky as product line (e.g. "Enki presenta Kinky Lubricants") | Clear product hierarchy. | Dilutes Enki brand. Kinky = one product. | ❌ Rejected |
| D) Kink & Co / Kinklab / Kinkhy | Avoid Meta "kinky" filter. | New brand = new DINAPI, IG, domain, customers. Lose Enki's 20K. | ❌ Rejected |
