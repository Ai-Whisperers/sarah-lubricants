### Key insights from Brazil

1. **Wholesale is the growth lever** — Shop Libido + Miess are wholesale-first, huge catalogs (1,000+ SKUs)
2. **Own-brand is possible** — PantyNova built a 8-figure brand on Instagram + influencer marketing
3. **Free shipping is table stakes** — all 7 top competitors offer free shipping above certain threshold
4. **Installment payments** — 10x or 12x sem juros (10–12 installments no interest) is universal
5. **Discreet packaging + private billing** — standard practice, expected by customers
6. **Influencer marketing works** — PantyNova built the brand with 50+ nano-influencers

### What Sarah can learn

- Build a **private-label SKU line** in 12+ months (PantyNova model) → higher margin
- Offer **installment payments** via Bancard or similar (PY equivalent) once e-com launches
- Wholesale expansion first → own brand later (PY → BR → UY sequence)

## Top 5 lessons for Sarah (PY)

1. **WhatsApp-first is a regional best practice** — confirmed by Bolivia + AR + PY all using it. Sarah's WA-first strategy is validated.
2. **Free shipping in capital + nominal fee to interior** is universal — Sarah's plan (free Asunción, Gs 25–50K interior) matches.
3. **Own-brand is the long game** — PantyNova built a multi-million USD brand from zero via influencer marketing. Sarah could do this in 12–24 months once PY distribution is solid.
4. **Wholesale is a 10x revenue lever** — Brazilian Shop Libido + Miess prove wholesale-first scales. Sarah's B2B outreach plan is correct.
5. **Influencer marketing works but needs discretion** — PantyNova's success was due to sex-positive framing without explicit content. Sarah's "wellness + cuidado personal" framing matches.
